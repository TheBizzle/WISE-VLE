Your investigation questions

In this activity you will use a more complex simulation to analyze the interaction between the airbag and the driver in a crash. You will be able to experiment with the driver's POSITION, VELOCITY, and DELAY TIME to address the following questions:

  • How does the position vs. time graph tell you whether the driver might have been harmed by the airbag?

  • How does the velocity vs. time graph tell you whether the driver might have been harmed by the airbag?

  • Why are shorter drivers at greater risk for harm from an airbag than taller drivers?

  • Is a driver more likely to be harmed by an airbag in a high speed or low speed collision?

  • How does the car's ability to "crumple" during a crash help prevent a driver from being harmed by an airbag?

Recall that the greatest risk for harm from an airbag comes from the driver striking an airbag that has not completely inflated. Record your thoughts on these questions in the next step.
