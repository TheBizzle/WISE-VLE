Some facts and figures?

Look at the following statistics on airbags, courtesy of the Insurance Institute for Highway Safety. Pay attention to who is at high risk in collisions involving airbags.



The National Highway Traffic Safety Administration estimates that airbags have saved the lives of more than 15,000 people.

However, from 1990 to 2004, 246 deaths reportedly have been caused by airbags inflating in low severity crashes. These deaths include 85 drivers, 11 adult non-drivers, 127 children, and 23 infants.

Of the 85 drivers killed by airbags, 64 were female, 21 were male. 58 were unbelted or improperly belted, and 24 were belted.

Of the 11 adult non-drivers killed by passenger airbags, 9 were females and 2 were males. 7 were unbelted or improperly belted, and 4 were belted.

Of the 127 children killed by passenger airbags, 122 were unbelted or improperly belted, and 5 were belted.

Starting with the 1998 model year, airbags typically deploy with less speed. Driver death rates are estimated to be 6 percent lower in 1998 and 1999 model passenger vehicles, compared with 1997 models.
