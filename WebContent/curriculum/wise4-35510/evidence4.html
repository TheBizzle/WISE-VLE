The chemical formulas on the left are reactants. The products of the reaction are shown on the right. The numbers in front of the formulas are called coefficients (the number '1' is usually omitted). In order to have equal numbers of each type of atoms, we need to have coeffients to balance the equation.

Scientists often use molecular models like the blue and green spheres to represent chemical reactions, and sticks to represent chemical bonds.
