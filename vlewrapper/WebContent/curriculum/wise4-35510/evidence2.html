Hydrogen As A Fuel

Hydrogen gas is colorless, odorless and tasteless. Each hydrogen atom (H) is made up of one electron and one nucleus. Each hydrogen gas molecule (H2) is made up of two hydrogen atoms (H). The two hydrogen atoms are connected by a H-H single bond.

Hydrogen can be produced from a variety of sources, such as water, and it can store the energy as chemical energy until needed. The chemical energy stored in one kilogram of hydrogen gas is three times the energy stored in one kilogram of gasoline.

Oxygen gas is also colorless, odorless and tasteless. Each oxygen atom (O) is made up of eight electrons and one nucleus. Each hydrogen gas molecule (O2) is made up of two oxygen atoms (O). The two oxygen atoms are connected by a O=O double bond.
